# ADR-002: 9-Zone Grid as the Pitch Model

**Date:** 2026-06-13
**Status:** Accepted

## Context

Several pitch zoning models exist in coaching literature: simple thirds, 5-lane
(Guardiola), horizontal bands by role, and the 9-zone thirds-plus-channels grid
used widely in FA coaching courses.

## Decision

Use the **9-zone grid**: the pitch divided into three horizontal thirds (attacking,
midfield, defensive) and three vertical channels (left, central, right), giving
9 named zones.

## Consequences

- **Pro:** Directly maps to FA grassroots coaching content — coaches using FA materials
  will recognise it immediately.
- **Pro:** Sufficient granularity to distinguish wide vs central play without overwhelming
  U12 players.
- **Pro:** Works cleanly with both 2-4-2 and 3-4-1 formations.
- **Con:** Does not model half-spaces (the areas between wide and central) which feature
  in advanced positional play coaching. This is acceptable at U12 level.
